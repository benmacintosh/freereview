John Duns Scotus said:
In an 'essentially ordered' series of events, every event is caused by the whole history of events
Events can't cause themselves, so there are no loops in these series
It is possible to hold 'perfect' causal power. This power would be neccesarily independant of past causes, i.e. it is a first cause
So it is possible there is a first cause
Then there must actually be a first cause, since otherwise it would be impossible for their to be a first cause

On the other hand, we could have an 'accidentally ordered series' in which each event only depends on the prior event (i.e. it is made of essential ordered series of size 2)
Each event then depends on the 'form' of this chain of events
This form is not part of the series (but like a family resemblance of events)
But then the accidental ordered series is dependant on a higher order essential cause, so it follows there is a first cause in this case also.
Are all events related accidentally or essentially?
